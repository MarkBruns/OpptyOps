# Ranking of the post-WWII U.S. Presidents, From Best To Worst

People admire FDR for his toughness after contracting polio, but the fact of the matter is that he was philanader who never really accomplished much of anything, although he absolutely was a snake in the grass and a master of deception, which is PART of why he got away the Presidency that was always two or three steps behind the curve, bumbling its way through a crisis and not providing genuine leadership and CAPABLE modern management. 

It's not JUST that modern presidents must operate in world in which nuclear weapons and sophisticated espionage and propagada first criminal outfits drive global terrorism exist, modern presidents also much navigate a hyper-partisan media landscape and a 24/7 news cycle that amplifies every misstep and magnifies every success, requiring them to be more politically astute and media-savvy than their predecessors who operated in a slower, less scrutinized environment. Earlier leaders, prior to the end of WW II or the start of the Cold War, just flat out ran simpler operations with fewer moving parts and less room for deep state weasels and people at the grass roots were less likely to be bamboozled by journalists and news media *explaining* and re-spinning the lies.

Trump entered office armed with much better firsthand knowledge of Washington’s entrenched resistance, allowing him to select a battle-tested cabinet and sidestep the early chaos and the use of traitorous leakers and political insider opportunists who destroyed Trump's first administration. In addition, the second term operates with a much sharper, edgier, more-advanced post-modern political operation honed by survival in unprecedented levels of attack. In spite of, or perhaps because of his [generally discredited] detractors, Trump 2.0 has delivered faster, more decisive wins on border enforcement and economic rebalancing without the constant legal and media distractions and traitorous leakers in the Cabinet.

2) Donald Trump’s first term, was just not remotely close to as good as his second term, but it still surpasses Ronald Reagan’s because it confronted China’s predatory trade practices head-on with tariffs and renogiated deals, like NAFTA, that reshaped global supply chains in ways that actually made better sense for everyone -- this is a MAJOR structural international tradechallenge Reagan never faced at anything like the scale of late 2010s international trade. Moreover, Trump secured the Abraham Accords—historic Middle East peace without new wars—while posting record-low unemployment across every demographic before the pandemic, which completely outpaced even the best parts of Reagan’s recovery in speed and inclusivity.

3) Ronald Reagan’s presidency outranks Dwight Eisenhower’s because his massive defense buildup and moral “evil empire” rhetoric help to accelerate a collapse of the USSR that had been underway since the 1970s. Reagan not only ended the Cold War, but ended it on American terms, even if his successor George H.W. Bush did not adequately capitalize on the opportunity and that failure set the stage for more missed opportunities throughout the 90s. Furthermore, Reagan’s supply-side tax cuts and deregulation shattered stagflation and helped Americans to believe in America again after the debacle of the Carter presidency and LBJ's VietNam, igniting two decades of dynamic growth and national optimism that Eisenhower’s steady but incremental post-war stewardship never matched.

4) Dwight Eisenhower surpasses Richard Nixon because he delivered what almost all Americans seemed to want: eight years of genuine peace, prosperity, and landmark infrastructure projects like the Interstate Highway System. Eisenhower did this while quietly, but steadfastly advancing civil rights without major scandals or disruptions to the bi-partisan agenda of Eisenhower and Senate Majority Leader Lyndon Johnson. In addition, Eisenhower’s calm, competent management of Cold War flashpoints preserved stability and working with all Parties, whereas Nixon’s Watergate scandal poured gas on the fires of public distrust and overshadowed Nixon's significant foreign-policy achievements.

5) Richard Nixon ranks above George W. Bush because his opening to China fundamentally reordered global geopolitics for generations. It also basically drove a stake into the heart of the USSR's dreams of worldwide communist monolith.  The Nixon / Kissenger policy of détente measurably lowered nuclear tensions and generally kept both the US and USSR from bankrupting themelves BEFORE exterminating each other in global thermonuclear war. It was not just in terms of the PRACTICAL and gigantic reductions in the threat of mutally-assured nuclear holocaust. Nixon was actually one of the most environmentally-responsible POTUSes, which was remarkable given that it was at a time when nobody seemed to understand the need for environmental stewardship. As Earth Day showed, there was a general eco-revolution brewing, ie people were tired of rivers catching fire, so co-opting the intensity of green extremists and creating a responsible, but minimalist EPA was pure Nixonian genius. Nixon also launched other launched major domestic reforms with such strategic precision, stepping into the breach but avoiding the prolonged Middle East quagmire and, might not have been decisive, but at least avoided the kinds of DISASTERS such as the late-term financial crisis that defined George W. Bush’s second term.

He also made a pathetic attempt to rein in the educational bureaucracy; "no child left behind" may have been a failure, but at least it was a genuine attempt at education reform which kept even worse things from happening [as under Obama or Biden]. In addition, Bush attempted to expand Medicare, although his attempt was old school RINO GOP material, a crony-based BigMedicine attempt and was not health-based or fitness-based, but instead gave Big Pharma a monsterous economic shot in the arm by giving worried seniors a prescription-drug benefit, for their Viagra, statins and other things they didn't need. In general, Bush had failings, but projected steady resolve that prevented further attacks on U.S. soil, achievements that greatly outpaced his father’s shorter, much less transformative term.

Bush outranks Gerald Ford only because of the elder Bush, as a famous WW II veteran, expertly assembled and led an international coalition to liberate Kuwait in the Gulf War while skillfully guiding the peaceful end of the Cold War and German reunification. Kennedy because his familiar, steady-hand leadership attempted to restore faith in the presidency after Nixon’s resignation and the Vietnam trauma. 9) John F. Kennedy ranks above Harry Truman strictly because of his [or his speechwriter's] rhetoric and his made-for-TV charismatic projection of a vision of what America could be. His highly fashionable leadership galvanized public opinion and generally made people believe that actual sci-fi moonshots were indeed feasible. JFK's calm resolve in the Cuban Missile Crisis, was a replay of his PT 109 heroism of WW II, and showed how leaders avert nuclear war without firing a shot. Moreover, Kennedy projected youthful idealism and vigor that renewed America’s global image, eclipsing Truman’s more controversial, unprepared, half-thought-through decisions such as how he handled the atomic bombings, or his disbanding of the OSS and failure to set up a genuine intelligence capability which defined the intelligence deficit that drove the stumbling into the Korean War stalemate.
