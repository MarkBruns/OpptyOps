# Reshaping Power Supply, Efficiencies, Data-center Needs For An AI Future

A 100-item list of near-commercial technologies and developments poised to dramatically reshape power supply, reliability, efficiencies, unique AI data-center needs, and overcoming different foreseeable constraints in related power technologies.

**1. NuScale VOYGR Small Modular Reactor**  
Description: Light-water PWR SMR with scalable 77 MWe modules that are factory-built and NRC-certified, enabling rapid deployment for baseload power. [Detailed explanation and design specs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_small_modular_reactor_designs) (and [NuScale official site](https://www.nuscalepower.com/)).  
This technology is important because it delivers factory-fabricated, passively safe nuclear power that can directly replace or supplement gas turbines for AI data center loads without grid dependence. It could be measurably significant by scaling to hundreds of MW per site within 3–5 years post-licensing, potentially supplying 10–20% of new U.S. data center demand by 2030. However, it is problematic because first-of-a-kind costs remain high, supply chain for modules is still ramping, and full commercial fleets face ongoing regulatory and financing hurdles that delay widespread rollout.

**2. GE Hitachi BWRX-300 Small Modular Reactor**
